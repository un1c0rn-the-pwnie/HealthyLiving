Body mass index (BMI) is a value derived from the mass (weight) and height of a person. The BMI is defined as the body mass divided by the square of the body height, and is expressed in units of kg/m^2, resulting from mass in kilograms and height in metres. The BMI is a convenient rule of thumb used to broadly categorize a person as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese based on tissue mass (muscle, fat, and bone) and height. The Nutritional status are based on metrics for adults over 20 years old

Activity:





Daily calorie burn is the total number of calories that your body expends in 24 hours, including all activities. It can vary widely in populations and is much higher for athletes or extremely active individuals. Caloric requirements may also vary among similarly active individuals due to differences in inherited metabolic rates. Daily calorie burn is determined with your basal metabolic rate (BMR) using multiple factors, including height, weight, age, and sex, then multiply the BMR by an activity factor to calculate. BMR is the total number of calories your body requires for normal bodily functions (excluding activity factors).

Note: These metrics are more of a guideline for a person to see the level of their well-being. Consult a doctor for more concrete information
